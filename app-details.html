About:

This project aims to create an intuitive navigation interface for young drivers who drive long-distance routes frequently that address a demonstrated market need for more personalized routes and stopping points, and the ability to save and share routes publicly or privately. These route personalizations could include helping users to find preferred stopping points in terms of brands/price, as well as integrating the ability to split the costs of gas between multiple travelers.

Reflection

This project was part of a User Interface Class Design course, where I collaborated with a teammate to design interactive application interfaces. Unfortunately, I faced challenges during that semester due to the impact of contracting Covid-19 and dealing with Bipolar disorder. As a result, I missed several in-class activities, which significantly contributed to a lower grade in the UX research process and, subsequently, a lower overall grade. I recovered from Covid and returned to class towards the end of the semester. Despite the setbacks, I contributed significantly to the UI design using Figma, based on the group's research. The outcomes of this contribution are displayed in the above picture section. I hesitated about showcasing this project, but considering that it marked the beginning of my understanding of UI/UX systems and helped me grasp the design process, I decided to share the part I contributed in UI. In preparation for the final deliverable in Figma design, I independently learned Figma. Having a foundation in Photoshop and Illustrator made it easy for me to pick up Figma, and it proved beneficial for my subsequent web design endeavors.
